Riveen Kumanayaka

Stories and Poems

The Scalmropia Archive

(Collection) A Collection of archival notes, leaked documents, story snippets, and royal decrees from Scalmropia. This is where much of the world-building is concentrated.

He Fought for You

(Poem) Mahmoud Darwish once wrote, "The wars will end and the leaders will shake hands, [...] I do not know who sold the homeland but I know who paid the price." This is for all those who paid the price.

Collided Galaxies

(Poem) Grief is often raw and remains a sad and tender reminder of the past. It is a soft ache that refuses to go away. Maybe in this cosmic dance, you may see someone else's stars embedded in your galaxy.

A Summer Night Under the Sky

(Poem) The stars hide a grand secret we are too stupid to know. But does it ever matter? Does anything hold meaning when you're drunk with love and run over by her beauty?

Masked Figures

(Poem) The pandemic, and the lonliness that stemmed from it, left a large imprint on all of us that survived it. This is for the horrors we all battled with ourselves and fear during then.

A Name I Didn't Think Of

(Short Story) This is a story of a slow-burn on fast-forward. He is a Physicist and she is a Philosopher, and they both end up finding that there's more that attracts than Gravity.

A Perfect Partner

(Poetry) Love often makes us see people in perfect lighting. It reaches a point where even things that used to irritate us, melts us on the inside; this is about the people who melts us.

Black and White

(Poetry) Morality is often thought as a strict "Black and White" concept. However, we must note that, in this world, there is no perfect black and white, but rather that everything is a shade of grey.

Death's Fear

(Poetry) Death is though to be the ultimate villain, the ghost that haunts over our shoulders. This is how death sees some people, and it's never about our pretty little lives.
