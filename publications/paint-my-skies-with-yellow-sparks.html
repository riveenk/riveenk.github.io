Paint My Skies With Yellow Sparks

Poem: War | 10 May, 2025


Trigger Warning: War, dehumanization, political violence  

In the loving cradle of relative safety
I tell the children of a tale down south.
They paint the skies with yellow sparks
And cheer all night with smoke around.

They have no children, no sick, no elderly—
They're only others, the cost of this art.
When they are gone, we’ll build a new home
And kick some dirt over the criminal's bones.

“Why?” they question, “why the yellow sparks?
We have no sparks, and we love them too.”
You are children. You are loved.
They are targets that must be crushed.

Yet they pester, with minds corrupt—
“They are human too,” they say.
“They would love to play with us
And paint our skies with yellow sparks.”

They know not of the others’ horror,
They speak in tongues foreign to us.
This home is ours, and they, a tenant;
The rent is due—and the price is death.
