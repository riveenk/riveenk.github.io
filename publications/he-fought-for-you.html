He Fought for You

Poem: War | Sheet 8


Trigger Warning: War, violence, death, and emotional trauma  

Crumbling concrete and the curséd sun
The scattered ashes of hope and laughter
Lifeless hands grabbing at the broken promises
A tuneless whistle haunting the air

Yet there still lies a warrior
Pleading to God, bargaining with the devil
For he waits for the smiles he left
That is his flower and his pod

It cost half a soul for him to leave
Leave home in mercy of others
At every tear he sheds
Every bullet that leaves his barrel
It costs another ever increasing tax

Every night, he stared into the stars, sleepless,
Constellations make up faces he sent to god
The tuneless whistle still plays at his ear
For the cost is hefty for a blossomed home

Every day, he danced to the tuneless whistle
And he realised, he'd been whistling all along
What a lonely sadist ambition is, making you
Sing through the screams and dance to its tune

He still fought to see another day, an
Ambiguous promise for a happily ever after
The air feels stale to breathe,
The flowers whisper a sob-tale

But the sob-tale hails the dead warrior’s soul
And the dead still dreams of what he lost:
Running home, seeing the sun rise tomorrow
Cheering on for the desperate victory
Holding their hands, warm and alive
Ignoring the corpses that lay beneath his feet
