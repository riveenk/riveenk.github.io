You Don't Come Closer

27 December, 2025

Frosty hints in the ruthless air,
No nights like this ever turned fair.
The trees are bare—it's almost December.
This eve will be one to remember.

You don't come closer, or hold my hand.
You pretend you don't notice a strand
begins to run down my face,
or sense that my heart has begun to race.

The coffee is cold, just like my mother.
It burns my lips when I beg it to smother
my racing heart with too much caffeine.
Yet fails to warm when the frost is mean.

The food is absent, just like my father.
It will arrive when the hunger doesn't bother
anymore and I'm half full with my cold coffee.
I can barely have anything bigger than a toffee.

You don't come closer, or flinch too hard.
But you tap your fingers like you'd guard
my scarred heart until it all healed
and no more of raw wounds stay revealed.

Am I bothering you? Is this too much?
I'd hate myself to use you as a crutch.
I've never told this much about me
to anyone, because there's always a fee.

You don't come closer, or tap your feet anymore.
You look down like you'd scrape the whole floor
just to collect the broken pieces of my soul
and glue it all together to make me whole.

The food arrives, a warm bowl of mac and cheese.
But I can't eat more and keep my belly at ease.
You can have it, maybe this is your fee.
I think I'll have a nice cup of tea.

I dread going home—it's almost December.
But this night has been one to remember.
I'll let you watch me finish my tea,
and secretly hope you, too, hate me.
