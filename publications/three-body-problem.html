Three Body Problem

04th March, 2026

From a distant frame pulling away from you
I see the flicker of your dancing figures
eclipsing against the dark starless sky,
as the growing minutes fill the space between us.

Each spin in your dance, his hand in yours,
tugs at the strings in the depths of my heart.
It's not love, or a magical charm.
It's the force of my heart breaking apart.

At nights when I think of you, I dream of a
system with just the two of us. Just our hands,
our gravity, and love, holding us together as
we spin about our common focal point.

But the cold chaos of the near-empty space
torments me with threats from corners afar.
He waltzes in to exchange hands, steal my dance,
and banish me from my own system.

My surface still bears traces of your face,
the light of your shine felt right in my eyes.
But your flickering lights fade away from my eyes
and your pull on my heart slips away from me.

I still think of you every now and then,
but you are too busy holding his hands.
You no longer feel the shades of my gravity
and I barely feel the pull from your heart.
