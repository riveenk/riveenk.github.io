The Nights Were Young

6 December, 2025 | Published in The Creator Magazine (8th Edition)

Aragalaya ("Struggle") was one of the most pivotal events I have ever witnessed and taken part in. It was an experience of both pride and fear alike. Ousting a family of war criminals and thieves, along with the toxic political world they built, cost more than just sweat and tears. Walking among brave protesters, trembling as buses burned nearby, I too lived through this period of change. Sri Lanka has always been my home at heart, and I am proud that I have fought for it. I'm glad I can share a part of our story through this poem.

Spring-touched breeze and a soothing sun—
the days were bright, but the nights were young.
Candles lit, their sins were sung.
The Asian Spring has just begun.

The wind will sing in freedom's voice
as we march in peaceful stride.
But alas! Our hearts have sighed,
for the wretched ones neglect our choice

sitting on their thrones of bones—yours and mine,
in their palaces—bound together in tears and blood,
lying drunk and dancing, as our sorrows flood,
in joyous tones, the damned lot, in old exotic wine.

Spineless fools sold their souls for a measly price
and forgot who fights for them. They think the foes
hold banners in hand, and concern in heart, but the world knows
the red scarfs have bargained the fools' pride for a pint and rice.

Blood was spilled, ours and theirs, both undeserved.
Frightened eyes watched khaki figures fight
their own kin, as the burning buses leaked cruel light.
Then night fell, hearts burning, yet too many unnerved.

We stand together as children of one
and watch the wretched Mynah fly.
Dance, my friends, better days draw nigh.
Under the soothing sun, rebirth has begun.
