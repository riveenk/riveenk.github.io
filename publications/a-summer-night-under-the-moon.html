A Summer Night Under the Moon

Poem: Romance | Sheet 8


The moon is out and in its distant glow
We reach out and find comfort and warmth
Me beside you, you beside me
We stare, hopeless, into the sky

Lunar beauty often traps the mind
Lures it away from the vast void of the sky
The human mind, quite helpless, falls
Victim to the mirage, divinely bright

In this tiny world, under the endless sky
Who are we to claim what's low and what's high
The moon from the earthly lands, seems as fire
Is to water, to the earth from selenian lands

Free from the pretence of beauty of luna
I keep staring into the darker night
Stars unknown and lands undiscovered
Wink in mockery of our feeble minds

What is love, in this grand scheme, what is life?
For all our tears and blood flow for a speck of dust
A fraction of a second in the scale of God
Is it to love and feel what is given
Or is it to venture out and find answers to questions unasked?

But, In this summer night with you beside me
Nothing makes sense
The moon that haunted romances of centuries
comes second to your beauty
And the universe that God willed to be endless
comes second to the love I have for you
So yes, the night's beautiful
But oh darling, how insignificant are we
In this baffling art of god, unfathomable?
