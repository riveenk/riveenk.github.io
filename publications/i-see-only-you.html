I See Only You

23 November, 2025

Trigger Warning: Death, Murder, Self-mutilation  

Your eyes, those beautiful blue eyes,
ever so vivid, even as the red lies
over your soaked dress, painting you
into the mess my mind outgrew.

In my mirror I see naught of myself
but the man who killed you: myself.
I wish I were gone, you put in my place,
but sin infects me with thoughts of disgrace.

No price is ever too great for you, I cry
as lifeless bodies on my table lie.
Skin tone, just like yours.
A round nose, just like yours.

If God exists, he'd watch me in pride
as I carve from myself my own bride.
Stitched together, mine for what's yours,
or to mimic what the truth lures.

My mismatched skin now glows in your tones.
A hint of you covers every inch of my bones.
Your arms so soft, so lovely, so slender,
a perfect model to carve my flesh tender.

to be you. An act of desperation that learns

lips, ears, and a head with a French braid.

My eyes are now two shades away from yours.
Every curve of my body was sketched from yours.
The stitches sting; each prick has a hint of blue.
I hope I'm hurting more than I hurt you.

Relics of bone and rotting decay in your
worship. The proof of my love lies on the floor
for you to see. I hope you see what I've done for you
for when I look in the mirror, I see only you.
